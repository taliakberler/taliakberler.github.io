About Me

Talia is currently a junior at the University of Miami pursuing a BS in computer science. She hopes to obtain a masters in computer science or data science in the future. She is passionate about her family and friends, her cats, soccer, and poetry.

Despite calling Miami home, Talia was born in Ithaca, NY and spent the first 8 years of her life in suburban Atlanta, GA. Due to financial circumstances caused by the 2008 recession, her family packed up and moved to Miami before she started 3rd grade at Coconut Grove Elementary.

Talia quickly excelled in school, being promoted to the gifted program for the remainder of elementary school. She then attended Ransom Everglades School for middle and high school, where her mother teaches history and her siblings and cousins also received their educations.

The 2020 pandemic struck in March of her senior year of highschool, resulting in a graduation on the couch and far too much time at home. This period did however provide ample time to explore her interest in poetry and writing.

Talia started college at Georgetown University, which remained online for the entirety of her freshman year. After finally living on-campus in the fall of 2021, a growing disillusionment with the school, in addition to health challenges and personal circumstances, led Talia to take a semester off to change course.

After a summer of reflection and growth, Talia now contentedly continues her studies at the University of Miami. She hopes to develop her data analytics abilities, in order to one day apply these skills to fighting inequality, promoting sustainability, and improving access to education.

Poetry

How to save your life

Most important, is the instruction manual:
how to, when to, with what, where
ever a stickler for rules I
followed each step, religiously

The scripture said:
Education!
so I beat knowledge into bloodshot eyes
while the blinking clock watched
Commitment!
so I dragged my beaten form to practice
meetings clubs councils dinners dances dates
Strength!
so I propped my empty mass on stilts
to animate the strained machine

But
Then
I broke down.
And there was no manual for that.

God vanished.
Religion failed.
Nothing mattered
matters
will matter,
Yet we live.

So I sought joy.

I fumigated the shattered body
filled it with poison to kill the sickness
moved intentionally fluidly with music and nature
was my cocoon my baptism the rain
performed my ablutions the birds sang
prayer electricity drew the ridges in the sky
sparked curiosity in the circuit board lit
the headlights and
I
saw
Truth.

The machine killed the soft animal,
structure was her cage.

From grief, I drew love;
that set us free.
